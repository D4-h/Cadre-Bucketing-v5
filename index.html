Project Overview

Public Service Recruitment Reform in Maharashtra

Our vision is to build a modern, tech-driven, and transparent recruitment system for the people of Maharashtra, truly realizing the goal of 'Requisition to Appointment' (मागणीपत्र ते नियुक्तिपत्र).

We are undertaking a landmark initiative to streamline the recruitment process for nearly 2,500 government cadres, creating a simplified and efficient path for over 15 lakh aspirants who apply each year.

We are undertaking a landmark initiative to streamline the recruitment process for nearly 2,500 government cadres, creating a simplified and efficient path for over 15 lakh aspirants who apply each year.

This reform will deliver a simple and fair system that significantly reduces delays and ensures that the most meritorious candidates are selected to serve the state of Maharashtra.

Identified Issues

Critical flaws identified in the existing recruitment rules that compromise the quality and fairness of hiring.

Illegible Physical Records

The reliance on poorly maintained and often illegible physical copies of recruitment rules makes it difficult to access and digitize criteria.

Preferences in Eligibility

Subjective clauses, such as preferring candidates with post-graduate degrees, complicate the selection process and can lead to inconsistencies.

Restrictive Institute Recognition

Recruitment rules that recognize qualifications from only one specific institute, limiting the pool of eligible candidates.

Solution: Cadre Bucketing - First Level

The strategic framework of "bucketing" to streamline and rationalize the ~2500 cadres. The first level of this framework involves a broad functional classification of all cadres into three primary categories: Administrative, Technical, and Developmental.

Solution: Cadre Bucketing - Administrative Domain

Bucketing for Administrative cadres, breaking them down into more specific domains. These sub-categories include General, Legal, Finance, Law Enforcement, and Other Admin to better group similar roles.

Solution: Cadre Bucketing - Technical Domain

Bucketing for Technical cadres, organizing them based on their area of expertise. The primary sub-groups include Engineering, Health, Sciences, and Mathematics/Statistical, among others.

Solution: Cadre Bucketing - Developmental Domain

Bucketing for Developmental cadres, which are grouped based on their functional roles in societal development. The key domains identified are Education and Social Welfare, with a third category for other developmental roles.

Solution: Guiding Principles for Bucketing

The four principal lenses, or criteria, that guide the cadre bucketing and recruitment rule analysis. The approach focuses on re-evaluating the relevance and verifiability of requirements related to Education, Experience, Skills, and Physical Qualifications
